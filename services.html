Patient Navigator Services

Navigating the medical system alone is overwhelming, especially with a rare or complex condition. I offer practical support based on real experience to help you feel informed, prepared, and less alone.

Who this is for

You don't need to have a rare disease to benefit from patient navigation. These services are for anyone who feels overwhelmed, unsure, or under-supported in their medical journey.

Patients

Newly diagnosed, facing treatment decisions, or managing an ongoing complex condition across multiple specialists.

Carers & families

Supporting a loved one through a complex medical journey and needing guidance on how to advocate effectively.

Rare disease community

Dealing with a condition where the research is limited, the specialists are few, and the path forward is unclear.

Where I help in your journey

A medical journey has many stages. I provide support at the moments that matter most.

Diagnosis

Understanding what you're facing and what it means

Decisions

Weighing treatment options and knowing what to ask

Second opinions

Finding the right specialist and validating your plan

Treatment

Coordinating care across specialists and staying on track

Ongoing care

Long-term management, monitoring, and advocacy

Services

One-off

Navigator Consult

A 45-minute one-on-one video call to talk through your situation, understand your options, and get clarity on the questions you should be asking.

  • Discuss your diagnosis, treatment options, and concerns
  • Help you understand what questions to ask your specialists
  • Talk through how to prepare for upcoming appointments
  • Share practical strategies for navigating the system
Best for: People early in a diagnosis, facing a treatment decision, or feeling overwhelmed and wanting to talk it through with someone who's been there.
Short engagement

Second Opinion Support

Practical help with the process of seeking a second opinion, from identifying the right specialist to making sure your records are in order.

  • Help identifying appropriate specialists for a second opinion
  • Guidance on preparing referral letters and organising records
  • Advice on what to ask and what to look for in a second opinion
  • Support navigating the logistics of interstate or specialist referrals
Best for: People who want to validate a diagnosis or treatment plan, or who feel they need a fresh perspective but don't know where to start.
Ongoing

Care Coordination

Ongoing concierge-style navigation support for people managing complex, multi-specialist care over time.

  • Keeping track of appointments, specialists, and the full clinical picture
  • Helping ensure nothing falls through the cracks across providers
  • Regular check-ins to review progress and adjust approach
  • Assistance with research and information gathering specific to your condition
Best for: People managing long-term, complex conditions that involve multiple specialists and ongoing treatment decisions.
